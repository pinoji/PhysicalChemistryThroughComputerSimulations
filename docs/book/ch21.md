# Thermodynamics of Mixing

## Chemical Potential for Ideal Gas

Obtain\...

$\star$ $\mu$ for pure ideal gas (mono atomic).

$\mu = \parfracii{A}{n}{T}{V} = -k_BT\parfracii{\ln z}{n}{V}{T}$

$Z = \frac{q^n(V,T)}{n!}$

$\ln Z = n\ln q -(N\ln N - N)$

$\frac{\partial \ln Z}{\partial n} = \ln q - \ln N - 1 + 1 = \ln \frac{q}{N}$

$$\begin{align*}
\mu &= -k_BT\ln \frac{q}{N}\\
&= -k_BT\ln \left(\underset{\uparrow}{\underbrace{\frac{q}{V}}}\frac{V}{N}\right) = -k_BT\ln\left(\frac{q}{V}\frac{k_BT}{P}\right)\\
&\text{\hspace{20pt}} \left(\frac{2\pi mk_BT}{h^2}\right)^{3/2} \cancel{V}/\cancel{V}\\
%&\frac{k_BT}{\underset{1 \text{barr}}{\underset{\downarrow}{P_0}}} = \underset{\searrow}{k_BT} \text{\hspace{20pt}} \nwarrow \text{ pure func of } T\\
&= -k_BT \ln \left(\frac{q}{V}k_BT\right) + k_BT\ln P\\
& \text{first term is a pure function of T}\\
&= \mu_0 + k_BT\ln P //
\end{align*}$$ $\star$ In case you are wondering what happens to the
unit inside of $ln$? Pressure is an energy per volume and it is unitless
in atomic unit. Now, we may want to make $\mu_o$ more useful by setting,
for example,$P=1$ATM.(This is same as shifting zero) In this case,
pressure unit conversion ($\times$ conversion factor inside $\ln$ is
included in $\mu_o$) Conveniently, this makes\
$$\begin{align*}
 \mu&=\mu_o + k_BT\ln P  \cdots \text{P in ATM}\\
& = \mu_o +0 - 0+ k_BT\ln P \\
& \text{with }  P_o=1\\ 
 & = \mu_o +\cancel{ k_BT\ln P_o} +  k_BT\ln {\left( \frac {P}{P_0} \right)}\\
\end{align*}$$

Note that $\frac{P}{P_o} =P$ in pure number. Special case, $P=1$ATM, we
denote $\mu_o$ with $\mu^{\ominus}$\

##  Mixing of Ideal Gas

$G = n_A(\mu_A^\ominus + k_BT\ln P_A) + n_B(\mu_B^\ominus+k_BT\ln P_B)$\
letting $x$ to be the mole fraction and using mole as a number unit\
with the help of partial pressure(General chemistry) $P_a=P \times x_A$\
$\Delta G = n_\text{tot} RT(x_a\ln x_a + x_b \ln x_b)$

Because mole fraction is always less than oe equal to 1, $\Delta G$ is
always negative for mixing process.\
$\Delta S = -\left(\frac{\partial \Delta G}{\partial T}\right) = -nR(x_a\ln x_a + x_b\ln x_b)$\
Similarly, entropy of mixing is always positive.
