#  Approximation Methods

We now consider system containing more than two electrons. Because
electrons are charged, they repel each other. Repulsion between nuclei
is easy to compute because they are represented by a point charge.
However, the electron density is a distribution determined by
$\left| \psi  \right|^2$. Calculating repulsive interaction between two
orbitals is performed by numerical integrations which are out of scope
for this textbook. Also, repulsive interactions introduce the additional
term in Hamiltonian; therefore, hydrogen-like atomic orbitals is not an
exact solution to the Schrödinger equation anymore. In other words,
$\psi$ becomes the unknown and central theme of quantum mechanics become
how to obtain an accurate representation of $\psi$ in terms of the
available basis. In this chapter, we introduce the fundamental
mathematical concept that is vital for obtaining the solution to the
Schrödinger equation.\

## Variational Principle

Ground state $\psi_0$ has the lowest energy denoted as $E_0$ by
definition

$$\begin{equation*}
\begin{split}
\hat{H}\psi_0 &= E_0\psi_0\\
\int\psi_0^\ast\hat{H}\psi_0dx &= E_0\int\psi_0^\ast\psi_0dx\\
\frac{\int\psi_0^\ast\hat{H}\psi_0dx}{\int\psi_0^\ast\psi dx} &= E_0
\end{split}
\end{equation*}$$

by def. any $\psi$ other than $\psi_0$ for non degenerate system\
$$\begin{equation*}
\frac{\int\psi^\ast\hat{H}\psi dx}{\int\psi^\ast\psi dx} = E > E_0
\end{equation*}$$

This is called \"variational principle\".\
This is super useful for chemistry because we do not know how to solve
Shrödinger Equation for any system having more than 1 electron.\
[i.e.]{.underline} He atom must be solved numerically using variational
theorem.

Simplest way to obtain approximate solutionis to ignore repulsion

$\phi_0(r_1, r_2) = \psi_{1s}(r_1)\psi_{1s}(r_2)$\

$\psi_{1s}(r) = \Big(\frac{Z^3}{\pi a_0^3}\Big)^{1/2}e^{-\frac{Zr}{a_0}}$,
$a_0 = \frac{4\pi\epsilon_0\hbar^2}{m_e e^2}$

These simple product wavefunction is often called Hartree product
wavefunction.\

$$\begin{equation*}

Note that Hamiltonian is not the simple Hamiltonian. So the solution is
not the optimum solution for normal Hamiltonian. This energy is higher
than the eigenvalue of exact wavefunction (-2.90 a.u.). So, variational
theorem is not violated.\

##  Secular Equation

So far, we have verified our trial wavefunction (simple hartree product)
is an eigenfunction and it did not violate the variational principle.
The Hartree product may be equivalent of the electron configuration we
studied in general chemistry.[^1] For example, He atom electron
configuration is $1s\alpha(1)1s\beta(2)$ following aufbau principle.
Because single hydrogen-like atomic orbital is not a good representation
of atomic orbital for multi-electron atomic system, we may represent
orbital by linear combination of hydrogen-like atomic orbitals.\
This time, we make $\psi$ more flexible.\
$\phi = \sum_{n=1}^N C_n f_n \hspace{10pt} \leftarrow$ linear
combination of some functions(i.e. hydrogen-like atomic orbitals)
