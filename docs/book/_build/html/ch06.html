Electron Spin (Quantum Mechanics Concept)#

Discovery of spin involved many experiments (Famous Stern-Gerlach experiment) and theories by many scientists including Kronig, Pauli and Dirac. Since physics relates to any of these is beyond the scope for this class, we simply accept existence of intrinsic spin on electron. Pauli’s exclusion principle as well as Hund’s rule follows as consequences of electron spin. Property of spin also suggest usage of a determinant to \(\psi\) as a simplest approximation.

Electron Spin Angular Momentum#

Each electron has an instrinsic form of angular momentum, called “spin”. \(\leftarrow\) If you’re interested in delving deeper into this topic, I recommend exploring the Stern-Gerlach experiment and related physics materials. It’s worth noting that intrinsic spin angular momentum cannot possess integer values, as there are no integer spins that would yield two distinct orientations, consistent with the experimental observation of electron deflection resulting in only two distinct spots. It turns out that \(1/2\) works for this case.
Just like angular momentum (associated with orbital \(\psi\) spatial)

\[\begin{split}\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \widehat{L}^2 Y_l^m\big(\theta, \phi\big) &= \hbar^2l\big(l + 1\big)Y_l^m\big(\theta, \phi\big)\\ \widehat{L}_z Y_l^m\big(\theta, \phi\big) &= m\hbar Y_l^m\big(\theta, \phi\big) \end{split} \end{equation*}\end{split}\]

\[\begin{split}\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \Delta L &= \pm 1\\ \Delta S &= 0\\ \Delta J &= 0, \pm1 \end{split} \end{equation*}\end{split}\]
